The Problem

Lead volume was up. Sales weren't impressed.

When I took over Durian FHC's media account in April 2025, the account was already running — Google Search, Pmax Store Visit, and Meta Lead Form campaigns were live across Mumbai and Thane. Lead volume looked fine on paper. Cost-per-lead looked fine on paper.

But the sales team's pipeline told a different story. A large share of "leads" reaching the CRM were dropping at the first call — wrong number, no budget, no real intent. The campaigns were optimised for the wrong thing: any form fill, not a person who'd actually walk into a showroom or have a real conversation with a designer.

The real fix wasn't a creative refresh or a bigger budget. It was redefining what counted as a win.

The Approach

Tracking leads through the actual sales funnel, not just the form submit

Instead of treating every Meta Lead Form or Google Pmax conversion as a finished result, I connected campaign performance directly to Durian's CRM stage data — mapping every single lead through what actually happened to it after the form was filled.

With this mapping in place, CPL became a secondary number — useful for budget pacing, but no longer the metric driving optimisation decisions. Interested leads and store walk-ins became the numbers that mattered, because they were the ones that correlated with actual revenue.

This changed three things about how the account was run:

  • Budget reallocation by quality, not volume. Keyword groups, audiences, and placements that produced high lead counts but low Interested-stage conversion got deprioritised — even when their CPL looked great.
  • Platform-level restructuring. Meta's split between Website Leads, Lead Form, Profile Visit, and Awareness campaigns was rebalanced based on which format actually pushed people to the Interested stage, not just which had the lowest cost-per-click.
  • City-by-city iteration. Mumbai and Thane were the original markets; Delhi, Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Noida were added progressively, each one benchmarked against the funnel data from the cities before it rather than starting from a blank slate.

The Result

CPL fell by 83% — as a side effect, not the goal

The irony: by stopping optimisation for CPL directly and optimising for funnel quality instead, CPL dropped anyway — because the campaigns got better at finding people who were actually going to take the next step.

The best single month on record was September 2025 — 250 leads at a blended ₹816 CPL, with a noticeably higher proportion reaching the Interested stage than in any prior month.

Across the full CRM dataset spanning the account's lifetime, campaigns sourced through Meta and the website have produced 540+ leads that reached the Interested stage and 50+ verified store walk-ins — the two numbers that now anchor every monthly review with the client, well above headline lead count or CPL.

What I'd Do Next

The funnel mapping is the playbook now

This approach — tying ad platform data to CRM stage progression instead of stopping at the conversion event — is now the lens I apply to every account I run, including Durian Furniture and Kotak Mutual Fund. A lead is only worth what happens to it after the click.
