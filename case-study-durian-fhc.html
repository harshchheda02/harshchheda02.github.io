The Problem

Lead volume was up. Sales weren't impressed.

I joined the Durian FHC account in February 2025, when the setup was deliberately simple: a single city (Mumbai), one Google Search campaign, and one Meta web-conversion campaign. That simplicity made sense at the start; the account needed to prove the channel worked before scaling spend or complexity.

But as spend increased through March, the same problem that surfaces on almost every lead-gen account appeared: lead volume looked healthy on paper, while the sales team's pipeline told a different story. A large share of "leads" reaching the CRM were dropping at the first call: wrong number, no real budget, no real intent.

The campaigns were optimised for the wrong thing: any form fill, not a person who'd actually walk into a showroom or have a real conversation with a designer. The fix wasn't a creative refresh or a bigger budget. It was changing what we measured.

The Approach

Tracking leads through the actual sales funnel, not just the form submit

Instead of treating every Meta Lead Form or Google Pmax conversion as a finished result, I connected campaign performance directly to Durian's CRM stage data, mapping every single lead through what actually happened to it after the form was filled.

Lead Form / Website Submit Top of funnel
↓
First Discussion Done Contacted
↓
Interested — primary KPI Real intent
↓
Store Walk-in — primary KPI Showroom visit
↓
Qualified Deal → Order Won Revenue

With this mapping in place, CPL became a secondary number, useful for budget pacing, but no longer the metric driving optimisation decisions. Interested leads and store walk-ins became the numbers that mattered, because they were the ones that correlated with actual revenue.

A note on attribution: paid vs. organic/website

A lead's CRM "Stage" only ever shows where a lead sits right now, not what it passed through to get there. To get an accurate picture, I track the full Stage History instead, logging every transition a lead ever made, which is the only way to correctly count something like "Interested" or "Digital Walk-in," since a lead can pass through that stage and later move on to a Qualified Deal, Closed Lost, or anywhere else.

Within that full history, not every lead can be cleanly attributed to a paid campaign. Leads tagged against an active Lead Form, Meta Website Camp, or Generic Camp are unambiguous — they only exist because of an active paid campaign. A meaningful share of the remaining volume comes through the website's organic enquiry form, direct visits, or untracked paths, which I exclude from the primary paid-attribution figure to avoid overstating what media spend alone produced.

This is also the exact bifurcation the client raised directly — they wanted organic contribution accounted for separately, not folded into "what the ads did." That split is now built into the monthly reporting cadence, pulled from the full stage-history log rather than a CRM snapshot.

For reference, total managed media spend across the account's lifetime (Feb 2025 – Jun 2026) is ₹32.4L+, generating 2,500+ total leads across all stages and sources combined.

What I Built On Top

A live dashboard, built off the clock

The manual pull across three sources (ad platforms, Zoho CRM snapshot, Stage History export) was taking too long every week. So I built a live dashboard that does the join automatically, using Google Sheets as the backbone and hosting it on Netlify.

Nine views: Paid Performance, Location walk-in rates with paid vs. organic split, Week-on-Week Trends, Drop Reasons (why and where leads go Closed Lost), Cost-per-Walk-in by location and month, Plan vs. Achieved against the media plan, Meta Creative performance by theme/format/category, Lead Quality scoring, and a deal-level Lead Explorer cross-referenced to the CRM. The whole thing is live. Refresh it anytime and it shows the current state of the account.

It got noticed. My manager and the client both called it out. I am building the same setup for Durian Furniture now.

What I Built On Top

A live dashboard, built off the clock

Every week, answering "how many walk-ins did paid campaigns actually generate this month" meant pulling from three separate sources: the ad platforms, a Zoho CRM stage snapshot, and a separate Stage History export — then joining them by hand. I built a dashboard to make that automatic.

The core design decision was using the full Stage History log instead of the CRM's current-stage snapshot. The difference matters: a lead that walked in and then later moved to "Quoted" or "Design Presentation Shared" disappears from the walk-in count in a snapshot view. The Stage History log preserves every transition, so the walk-in count is always accurate regardless of where the deal ended up.

It tracks 18 pipeline stages across 6 store locations and 3 platforms (Google, Meta, Organic), with Cost-per-Walk-in as the primary efficiency metric. Nine views: Paid Performance, Location walk-in rates with paid vs. organic split (showing where organic out-pulls paid and vice versa), Week-on-Week Trends, Drop Reasons (13 categories — Budget Concern, No Response, Wrong Number, etc., by location and furthest stage before Closed Lost), Cost-per-Walk-in by location and month, Plan vs. Achieved against the media plan, Meta Creative performance by Category/Format/Theme/Creative Set, Lead Quality funnel by location and platform, and a deal-level Lead Explorer searchable by deal number and owner.

It connects live to Google Sheets — open it anytime and it shows the current state of the account. My manager and the client both called it out. Building the same setup for Durian Furniture now.
